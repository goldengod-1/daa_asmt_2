Comparative Analysis

The algorithm aims to predict the most stable secondary structure based on base-pairing rules (A-U, U-A, C-G, G-C), which may not always match the actual expected structure.
The algorithm may predict more pairs than the actual expected RNA structure due to its optimization strategy. It prioritizes maximizing the number of base pairs within the sequence,
which can lead to overestimation, especially in regions with multiple potential pairings. In some cases, the algorithm may identify pairs that are not present in the actual expected structure,
resulting in a higher count of predicted pairs compared to the actual structure. While the algorithm aims to find the most stable secondary structure based on optimization criteria,
it may not always reflect the biological reality accurately. Biological RNA structures are influenced by various factors such as tertiary interactions, folding kinetics, and RNA-binding proteins,
which are not fully captured by the algorithm's optimization approach.
